# Washington, DC Trip Plan

## Trip Window

- Destination: Washington, DC
- Origin: Seattle
- Trip length: 5 days / 4 nights
- Assumed year: 2026
- Hotel check-in: Saturday, May 23, 2026
- Hotel check-out: Wednesday, May 27, 2026
- Preferred airport: DCA if price is close to IAD
- Transportation: No rental car, use Metro + walking

## Why This Version

This version keeps the trip compact while still covering the main Memorial Day weekend events and the core DC highlights without trying to do too much.

## Lodging Notes

- Aim for a budget stay in Penn Quarter, Chinatown, Downtown, Foggy Bottom, Dupont, or U Street.
- Budget-friendly options previously discussed:
  - Motto by Hilton Washington DC City Center
  - Hotel Hive
  - Generator Hotel Washington DC
  - U Street Capsule Hostel
- If flying out on May 27, the correct hotel checkout date is May 27, 2026.

## Public Transit Notes

- DC is workable without a car.
- DCA is the easier airport for public transit.
- Metro is suitable for this trip, with extra walking on National Mall days.
- Georgetown is the least convenient area by Metro, but still reachable by bus or a longer walk.

## Day-by-Day Plan

## Day 1: Saturday, May 23, 2026

- Fly from Seattle to Washington, DC
- Check in to hotel
- Keep the first day light
- Evening walk if energy allows:
  - White House area
  - Penn Quarter
  - Short dinner nearby

## Day 2: Sunday, May 24, 2026

- Major museum day
- Best option:
  - National Air and Space Museum if timed entry is available
- Backup option:
  - American History Museum
- Evening:
  - National Memorial Day Concert

## Day 3: Monday, May 25, 2026

- Memorial Day focus
- Main event:
  - National Memorial Day Parade
- After parade:
  - Lincoln Memorial
  - Vietnam Veterans Memorial
  - Korean War Veterans Memorial
  - World War II Memorial
- If energy remains:
  - Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial
  - Franklin Delano Roosevelt Memorial

## Day 4: Tuesday, May 26, 2026

- Museum / civic sights day
- Recommended:
  - National Museum of American History
  - One additional nearby Smithsonian museum
- Alternative if you want a more special weekday stop:
  - Library of Congress

## Day 5: Wednesday, May 27, 2026

- Easy final morning
- Breakfast and short neighborhood walk
- Optional quick stop if flight timing allows:
  - National Portrait Gallery
  - Dupont Circle
- Check out and head to airport

## Core Priorities

If time gets tight, prioritize these:

1. Lincoln Memorial
2. National Memorial Day Parade
3. One Smithsonian museum
4. National Memorial Day Concert
5. World War II Memorial and nearby memorials

## Booking Checklist

- Book flight from Seattle
- Prefer DCA over IAD if fares are similar
- Book hotel for May 23 to May 27, 2026
- Reserve timed entry if needed for:
  - Air and Space Museum
  - Library of Congress

## Budget Approach

- Keep lodging simple and near Metro
- Skip rental car
- Use Metro and walk
- Use rideshare only if arriving very late or if legs are done for the day

## Summary

This is a focused first DC trip built around Memorial Day weekend. It is short enough to stay manageable and long enough to avoid turning the trip into a rush.
